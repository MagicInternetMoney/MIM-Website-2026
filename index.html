Lore and narrative

The joke was early. The spell was real.

Magic Internet Money, or $MIM, began around 2014 as a playful altcoin experiment by Christopher Franko, long before memecoins became a market category.

The name nodded at the way skeptics described Bitcoin in the forum era: “What’s Bitcoin?” “Magic internet money, bro.” MIM leaned into that absurdity and turned it into a banner for crypto’s weirdest superpower.

It was mineable, whimsical, and deliberately unserious in a scene that often tried very hard to look serious. The point was not only the coin. It was the belief machine: community, code, humor, and culture coordinating in public.
